SPLM was founded on the belief that software product teams deserve better tools — tools that match how engineering and product actually work, not how project management theory says they should.

Our mission

Reduce the distance between idea and delivery

Every day, product teams lose hours to status meetings, scattered spreadsheets, and miscommunication between engineering and business. SPLM exists to eliminate that waste. We give teams one place to capture requirements, plan work, and track releases — so they spend their time building instead of coordinating.

We believe the best teams in the world are not necessarily the ones with the largest budgets or the most experience. They are the ones with the clearest systems. SPLM is that system.

Our story

We've been on your side of the table

SPLM was built by engineers and product managers who spent years dealing with the same problems — tracking features in spreadsheets, managing releases over email, and losing critical context when engineers and product teams weren't in the same room.

We knew there had to be a better way. So we built it. SPLM was designed from day one around how modern software teams actually work: asynchronous, cross-functional, and always moving fast.

Today, SPLM is trusted by product teams at companies of all sizes — from early-stage startups shipping their first product to enterprise organizations managing dozens of concurrent releases.
